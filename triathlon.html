Daily Schedule

Morning

6:45 AM — Wake
Collagen + Vitamin C
15 g hydrolyzed collagen powder mixed in plain water + one 50 mg vitamin C tablet. Nothing else — this is a supplement, not a meal. Collagen peptides reach peak blood amino acid levels approximately 60 min after ingestion (Shaw et al., 2017), so taking it now means it is available when you load tissue at the gym. Note: collagen gummies typically provide only 2.5–5 g per serving, well below the 15 g minimum used in clinical research. Use powder.
6:50 – 7:15 AM
Morning Ritual (at home)
1. Heat hip-glute junction (left side) — heating pad or hot towel, 10 min while getting ready. Heat increases tissue extensibility before stretching.
2. Lacrosse ball glute med release — 2 min per side. Lie on ball at the tender spot, hold on sore points 30–60 sec. Use moderate pressure — this should not be excruciating.
3. Stretch circuit — hold 30 sec × 2 reps each: Figure-four → 90-90 hip → Standing IT band (cross leg behind, lean away) → Kneeling hip flexor/TFL lunge → Pigeon pose.
7:15 AM
Light Pre-Gym Fuel (150–200 cal)
A banana + small handful of nuts, OR a few berries + 1 tbsp almond butter, OR half an apple + 10 almonds. Keep it small — last night's dinner maintains glycogen for a morning session. A full meal this close to training is unnecessary and increases discomfort.
7:45 AM
Gym
Strength or cardio per programming. Begin every session with the glute activation warm-up for your current phase (see Training Plan).
~9:15 AM
Post-Gym Breakfast
This is the main morning meal. Smoothie (berries + protein powder) + 1 whole egg + 2 egg whites scrambled with spinach. Drizzle 1 tbsp extra virgin olive oil on the eggs. Take NAC (600 mg) with this meal. Target: ~35 g protein, ~40 g carbs.

Afternoon & Evening

12:30 PM
Lunch — Tiffin Meal
Healthy tiffin (protein + veggie + side + probiotic). Choose the highest-protein option available. Add Greek yogurt (unsweetened) on the side if protein feels light. Target: 30–40 g protein. For omega-3 intake, substitute sushi 2–3×/week — prioritize salmon (sake), tuna (maguro), or mackerel (saba) as nigiri or sashimi. Avoid tempura rolls (fried).
3:30 PM
Snack
Protein shake or Greek yogurt (unsweetened) + handful of walnuts or almonds. ~20 g protein.
7:00 PM
Dinner — Tiffin Meal + Additions
Second tiffin meal (high-protein option). Add: Greek yogurt (unsweetened) on the side + 2–3 tbsp fermented vegetables (sauerkraut or kimchi) + drizzle 1 tbsp extra virgin olive oil over food. Take curcumin (1 g with black pepper/piperine for absorption) with this meal.
8:15 PM
Last Meal — Pre-Bed Snack
Cottage cheese (1 cup) or Greek yogurt with chia seeds. 10–20 g dark chocolate (85%+ cacao). Optional second curcumin (1 g). ~25 g slow-digesting protein. Finish eating by 8:30 PM — 2 hours before sleep.

Night Ritual — ~9:00 to 10:15 PM (~15 min active)

Step 1
Ice lateral knee — 15 min
Wrap ice in cloth — never directly on skin. Drop this step once knee is no longer tender to touch (typically by Week 3–4).
Step 2
Foam roll — 60 sec per muscle group
Quads, hamstrings, calves, TFL. Avoid rolling directly on the IT band or the tender hip-glute junction — roll the muscles around them instead.
Step 3
Lacrosse ball glute med release — 2 min/side
Same technique as morning. Moderate pressure, hold sore points.
Step 4
Stretch circuit — same 5 stretches as morning
Figure-four → 90-90 → Standing IT band → Hip flexor/TFL lunge → Pigeon. Take magnesium (200–400 mg) after stretching.
10:30 PM
Lights out
Aim for 7.5–8 hours of sleep. Sleep quality directly affects tissue repair, hormone regulation, and training adaptation.
🧊
Ice/Heat reference: Lateral knee → ICE after activity (reduces inflammation). Hip-glute junction → HEAT before stretching (increases tissue extensibility). Avoid using the massage gun directly on the tender hip spot or on the IT band — use it on surrounding muscles (quads, calves, upper glutes) only.

14-Week Training Plan

Existing schedule (3 strength + 2 cardio) stays. This plan specifies what to add, swap, or modify within those sessions.

Phase 1 · Weeks 1–2

Calm & Activate

No running. No cycling unless pain-free.

Add to every strength session as warm-up:
Clamshells 3×15 · Glute bridges (double leg) 3×12 · Side-lying leg raises 3×12 · Prone hip extensions 3×10

Cardio days: Swap running → swimming 3–4×/week, 20–30 min. Elliptical or pool running also acceptable.

Strength note: Avoid heavy barbell squats and conventional deadlifts during this phase. Replace with goblet squats (above parallel) or moderate leg press. Core work: dead bugs, bird dogs, Pallof press — avoid sit-ups and weighted crunches.

Advance when: Pain-free 30-min walk. Clamshells feel easy at bodyweight. Lateral knee tenderness is significantly reduced.

Phase 2 · Weeks 3–4

Build Foundation

Still no running. Cycling begins if pain-free.

Add as accessory work:
Banded clamshells 3×15 · Single-leg glute bridges 3×10/side · Lateral band walks 3×12/direction · Bodyweight mini squats to 45° 3×12 · Step-ups on 6–8″ box 3×10/side · Side plank 3×20s/side

Swim: 3–4×/week, 30–40 min. Start 50m interval repeats.
Cycling: Easy 2×/week, 20–30 min, flat terrain.

Advance when: Pain-free 30-min bike. 15 single-leg bridges per side without difficulty. All exercises completed without lateral knee discomfort.

Phase 3 · Weeks 5–6

First Running

One cardio day becomes a run day.

WeekProtocolFreq
51 min run / 2 min walk × 7 rounds = 21 min2×/wk
62 min run / 1.5 min walk × 7 rounds = 24 min2×/wk

Cadence target: 170+ spm. Use a metronome app. Higher cadence reduces over-striding, which reduces IT band compression at the knee (Willy, 2022). Never schedule back-to-back run days.

Strength additions: Forward lunges 3×10/side · Single-leg RDL 3×8/side · Split squats 3×10/side · Resisted crab walks 3×12/direction. Continue all Phase 2 exercises.

Swim: 4×/week, 35–45 min. Start open water practice if the triathlon is open water.
Cycling: 2–3×/week, 30–45 min, gentle hills.

🛑
Stop rule: If lateral knee pain reaches 2/10 or higher during any run → stop running and walk home. Take 2–3 days off running (continue strength work), then retry at reduced volume.

Advance when: Two consecutive weeks of walk/run with zero lateral knee pain during or within 24 hours after.

Phase 4 · Weeks 7–8

Build Run Volume — Maximise Swim

WeekProtocolFreq
73 min run / 1 min walk, 30 min total3×/wk
85 min run / 1 min walk, 30 min total3×/wk

Increase total running distance by no more than 10% per week. These are the last full weeks of pool access — prioritise swim volume now.

Swim: 4–5×/week, 40–50 min. Include race-pace efforts and brick workouts (swim→bike or swim→run) 1×/week.
Cycling: 2–3×/week, 45–60 min with hills and tempo efforts.

Strength: Split squats with light DB/KB 3×10/side · Single-leg squats to box 3×8/side · Bulgarian split squats 3×8/side · Heavier band walks 3×15/direction · Single-leg calf raises 3×12/side · Side plank with hip dip 3×10/side.

Advance when: Can run 15 min continuously with no pain. Single-leg squat to 45° is controlled and stable.

Phase 5 · Weeks 9–10

Continuous Running — Final Pool Sessions

Swim (last 1–2 weeks of access): 5×/week, 45–60 min. Include full race-distance swims. Brick workouts 2×/week to practice transitions.

Strength drops to 2×/week (maintenance). Add: controlled lateral skaters 3×8/side — first plyometric exercise, so start conservatively at 2×6. If any hip or groin discomfort occurs, substitute with lateral lunges.

Phase 6 · Weeks 11–12

No Pool — Bike & Run Focus

Swim replacement (2×/week dryland): Band lat pulldowns, band pull-aparts, prone Y-T-W raises. Use any available open water even 1×/week.

Cycling: 3–4×/week, 60–90 min. One long ride 75–90 min. Brick workout: bike→run 1×/week.
Do not reduce strength training below 2×/week.

Phase 7 · Weeks 13–14

Peak + Taper

Week 13 (peak): Longest run 35–40 min · longest bike 90 min · full brick race simulation · one tempo/race-pace effort in both bike and run · strength 1–2×/week at reduced volume (2 sets instead of 3–4).

Week 14 (taper): Cut training volume by 40–50%. Short easy runs only (15–20 min). Easy bike spin (30 min). No heavy strength — continue morning ritual and glute activation only. Rest 1–2 full days before race. Night ritual continues unchanged.

Nutrition Plan

⚡
Do not cut calories during recovery. A caloric deficit slows tendon repair, suppresses thyroid function, and raises cortisol (Tipton, 2015). Eat at maintenance or a slight surplus through at least Week 10.

As Training Volume Increases (Weeks 5+)

  • Post-gym breakfast gets bigger: add oats or toast to increase carbs
  • Request larger carb portions from tiffin service (extra rice, dal, or roti)
  • Add a mid-morning snack if needed (Greek yogurt + berries)
  • During sessions longer than 60 min: 30–60 g carbs/hour (sports drink, gel, banana) — this trains the gut for race day
  • Post-brick workout: 0.8 g/kg carbs + 20–30 g protein within 30 min

Race Week (Week 14)

Carb loading 2–3 days before: Increase carb-dense foods at every meal (white rice, pasta, potatoes). Reduce fibre slightly. Hydrate with electrolytes.

Race morning (3–4 hrs before): Familiar carbs only — white rice with banana, oatmeal, toast with almond butter. Nothing new on race day.

During race: Swim — no fueling. Bike — 60–90 g carbs/hour (primary window). Run — 30–60 g/hour.

Daily Food Priorities

  • Extra virgin olive oil: ~2 tbsp/day across meals — contains oleocanthal, which has documented anti-inflammatory properties. Use as cooking fat and finishing drizzle.
  • Berries: daily in smoothie or as snack. Eat whole, not juiced.
  • Fermented vegetables: 2–3 tbsp sauerkraut or kimchi with dinner — supports gut microbiome diversity
  • Dark chocolate (85%+): 10–20 g — provides flavanols that support circulation
  • Nuts and seeds: walnuts, flaxseed, chia — plant-based omega-3 and zinc sources
  • Sushi or fatty fish: 2–3×/week for EPA/DHA omega-3s. Choose nigiri or sashimi over tempura.

Limit or Avoid

  • Processed foods — minimise as much as practically possible
  • Seed oils high in omega-6 (corn, soybean, sunflower, canola) — use extra virgin olive oil or avocado oil
  • Added sugar and fruit juice — sports drinks only during sessions >60 min
  • Alcohol — impairs tissue healing and sleep quality
  • Fried foods

Training Rules & Red Flags

Rules to Follow Throughout the Plan

  1. 01Morning ritual every day. Heat, release, stretch, activate — including rest days and race day.
  2. 02Hip strengthening 2×/week minimum, permanently. Runners who stop glute work after symptoms resolve are the most likely to relapse (Fredericson et al., 2000).
  3. 0310% rule. Never increase weekly running volume by more than 10%, even if you feel good.
  4. 04Cadence 170+ steps per minute on every run. Reduces over-striding and lowers IT band compression at the knee.
  5. 05No back-to-back high-intensity days. A hard run followed by a hard bike the next day counts as consecutive loading.
  6. 06Replace running shoes every 300–500 miles. Worn midsoles alter ground reaction forces and gait mechanics.
  7. 07Exhale on exertion during strength training. Avoid holding breath under load (Valsalva maneuver).
  8. 082/10 pain rule. If lateral knee pain reaches 2/10 during any run: stop, walk home, take 2–3 days off running (continue strength), then retry at reduced volume.

Red Flags — Consult a Professional

🚨
Seek evaluation from a sports physiotherapist or orthopedic specialist if any of the following occur:
• Knee pain has not improved at all by Week 4
• Pain is worsening despite adherence to the plan
• Numbness, tingling, or pain radiating down the leg
• Hip junction pain becomes sharp during walking
• Unable to perform a pain-free single-leg squat to 45° by Week 6
• Any new or worsening symptoms in the groin or hip region during exercise
