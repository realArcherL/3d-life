Phase 7 · Weeks 13–14 · Race Prep

Week 14 taper: Cut volume 40–50% · Short easy runs 15–20 min · Easy spin 30 min · No heavy strength — morning ritual only · Rest 1–2 days before race · Continue night ritual unchanged.

Permanent Bulletproofing Rules

  1. 1.Morning ritual every day. Heat, release, stretch, activate. Even race day.
  2. 2.Hip strengthening 2×/week minimum forever. Maintenance, not rehab.
  3. 3.10% rule. Never increase weekly running volume more than 10%.
  4. 4.Cadence 170+ spm. Shorter, quicker steps reduce IT band load per stride.
  5. 5.No back-to-back hard days. Hard run → hard bike = consecutive loading.
  6. 6.Replace running shoes every 300–500 miles.
  7. 7.Exhale on exertion. Never hold breath under load. Protects athlete past issues *.
  8. 8.2/10 pain rule. Lateral knee ≥2/10 during any run: stop, walk home, 2–3 days off, retry at reduced volume.

Red Flags — Refer to Professional

  • Knee pain has not improved at all by Week 4
  • Pain is getting worse despite following the plan
  • Numbness, tingling, or pain radiating down the leg
  • Hip junction pain becomes sharp with walking
  • Cannot do a pain-free single-leg squat to 45° by Week 6
  • Any increase in groin pressure or sharp groin pain during exercise related to athlete past issues *

Nutrition Plan

Priorities (In Order)

  1. Do not cut calories during recovery. Healing tissue requires energy. Calorie deficit slows tendon and connective tissue repair.
  2. Protein elevated — rebuilding tissue AND training.
  3. Carbs periodised — match training load week by week.
  4. Anti-inflammatory foods — support IT band and athlete past issues *.
  5. Collagen + vitamin C — strongest evidence-based intervention for tendon recovery.

Calorie Rule

Eat at maintenance or a very slight surplus during Phases 1–5. Baseline: 30 kcal/kg body weight + 5–10 kcal/kg/day for tissue repair. Only consider a modest deficit (300–500 cal max) in the final 2 weeks if needed, once healing is well established.

During Training / Around Workouts

  • Pre-workout (60–90 min before): 1–2 g/kg carbs + moderate protein (oatmeal + banana + protein)
  • Post-workout (within 30 min): 0.5 g/kg carbs + 20–30 g protein (shake + fruit, or chicken + rice)
  • During sessions >60 min: 30–60 g carbs/hour (sports drink, gel, banana). Sprint race may not need mid-race fueling (45–90 min).

Race Week Nutrition

2–3 days before race (carb load): White rice, pasta, bread, potatoes at every meal. Reduce fibre slightly. Keep protein at 1.4–1.6 g/kg. Hydrate aggressively with electrolytes.

Race morning (3–4 hrs before): Familiar, easy-to-digest, carb-rich meal — white rice with honey, plain toast with jam, banana. Small protein. Nothing new on race day.

During race: Swim — no fueling. Bike — 60–90 g carbs/hour (primary fueling window). Run — 30–60 g carbs/hour, whatever gut tolerates. Practice this in training.

Anti-Inflammatory Foods (Daily Priority)

  • Extra virgin olive oil: 2 tbsp/day minimum — primary cooking and finishing fat (Blueprint non-negotiable)
  • Fatty fish (salmon, mackerel, sardines): 2–3 servings/week
  • Berries (blueberries, strawberries, blackberries): eat daily, not juiced
  • Leafy greens: spinach, kale, arugula daily
  • Cruciferous vegetables: broccoli, cauliflower, Brussels sprouts
  • Curcumin/turmeric: 1 g supplemental with black pepper (with dinner, optionally before bed)
  • Fermented foods: 2–3 tbsp daily of sauerkraut or kimchi
  • Nuts and seeds (walnuts, flaxseed, chia, hemp): omega-3, zinc, fibre
  • Dark chocolate 85%+: 10–20 g daily for flavanols

Foods to Eliminate or Strictly Limit

  • Processed foods — eliminate or maximum 10%
  • Seed oils high in omega-6 (corn, soybean, sunflower, canola) — replace with EVOO and avocado oil
  • Added sugar in all forms — no fruit juice, no sweetened yogurts, no sports drinks outside sessions >60 min
  • Alcohol — impairs tissue healing, sleep, and increases inflammation
  • Fried foods and processed meats

Additional Nutrition Notes (athlete past issues *)

  • Fibre 25–30 g/day prevents constipation (reduces intra-abdominal pressure)
  • Hydrate to pale-yellow urine all day (supports circulation and venous return)
  • Avoid bloating triggers before training: large meals, carbonated drinks, excess raw cruciferous veg. Eat big meals ≥90 min before gym.

Connective Tissue Protocol & Supplement Stack

⭐
Collagen + Vitamin C is the strongest evidence-based nutritional intervention for tendon recovery. GRADE A evidence from systematic review of 8 RCTs for increases in tendon cross-sectional area and stiffness at 15–30 g/day paired with resistance training.

Collagen Protocol

15–30 g hydrolysed collagen peptides + 50 mg vitamin C, taken 30–60 minutes before rehab exercises or morning ritual. Use a vitamin C tablet or whole food (strawberries, bell pepper) — NOT juice, which spikes blood glucose. Collagen is a targeted supplement, NOT a protein substitute.
