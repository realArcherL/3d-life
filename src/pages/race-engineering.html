Swim — 750m Open Water
🏊
Race strategy: The swim is about survival and positioning. You cannot win the race here, but you can lose it by going out too hard. Sight the buoys every 6–8 strokes. Draft beside a swimmer going your pace if possible.
Segment Effort Instruction
Start → 200m Tempo Controlled start. First 200m will feel chaotic. Find your rhythm, don't sprint. Settle into your stroke within the first 100m. Sight the first buoy early.
200m → 550m Steady Settle and draft. Maintain steady aerobic effort. Efficient stroke, bilateral breathing. Keep buoys on your LEFT. If you can draft, tuck behind someone's hip.
550m → 750m Steady Finish controlled. Do NOT sprint the last 200m — you need your legs for 12.5 miles of bike. Kick a bit harder in the last 100m to get blood flowing to your legs for the beach run to T1.
Bike — 12.5 mi Out & Back
🚴
Race strategy: Exit T1, turn LEFT (south) onto Knoxville Rd. Outbound 6.2 mi is net uphill (+30m). Return is net downhill. Ride at 80–85% effort. The biggest mistake in sprint tri is riding too hard and having nothing left for the 5K.

Run — 3.1 mi Out & Back
🚨
IT BAND RACE STRATEGY: The outbound 1.55 mi is almost entirely UPHILL (+50m) — safer for your IT band but harder on cardio. The return 1.55 mi is almost entirely DOWNHILL (−50m) — the danger zone for IT band compression. Maintain 170+ spm cadence on the descent. Shorten your stride. Do not overstride downhill. Race Day Nutrition Timing
⏱
Sprint tri total time: ~1:00–1:30. Short enough that you won't bonk if you fuel properly beforehand, but the bike effort plus the climbing run means you need SOME fuel during the race. Goal: start the run with full glycogen and stable blood sugar.
Race Morning −3 hours
Carb-rich breakfast: white rice + banana, oatmeal, or toast with almond butter. 1–2 g/kg carbs. Collagen + vitamin C. 500ml water with electrolytes.
Race Morning −30 min
Small top-up: half a banana or 2–3 dates. Sip water. No large meals. Apply sunscreen.
Swim → T1
Nothing. Just get to your bike fast. Quick sip of water if needed.
Bike Mile 1–2
Small sips of water or electrolyte drink. Settle in before eating.
Bike Mile 4–5 (descent)
Take a gel or chews HERE. Low effort — stomach can handle it. 20–30g carbs. Chase with water. Will be in your bloodstream by the run climb.
Bike Mile 10–12 (coast to T2)
Finish your water bottle. Nothing solid — it won't absorb before the run starts.
Run — Turnaround (mi 1.55)
Grab water at the aid station. Drink or pour on your head. Do not skip this.
Finish Line
Rehydrate immediately. Protein shake within 30 min. Collagen + vitamin C within 60 min. Full meal within 2 hours.
Race Day Non-Negotiables
1Cadence 170+ spm on the entire run. Especially miles 1.55–2.5 (the descent). Shorter steps = less knee flexion = less IT band compression per stride.
2If lateral knee pain hits 1/10 during the run descent: walk. Walk 30 seconds, retry with even shorter strides. If it returns: walk/jog to the finish. A slow finish is better than a 6-week setback.
3Spin 100+ rpm with zero resistance for the last mile of the bike. Flushes lactic acid and pre-activates the neuromuscular patterns for running.
4Do not stand-climb on the bike. Seated climbing only. Standing loads the IT band and fatigues your glutes faster.
5Do not sprint the last 200m of the swim. Kick harder to activate your legs, but don't blow up your heart rate. The bike climb starts at mile 1.
6Take the gel at bike mile 4–5. The descent is your fueling window. The carbs will be available by the run climb at mile 1.0.
7Morning ritual on race day. Heat, lacrosse ball release, stretch circuit — same as every other day. Do not skip it. Arrive early enough.
8Nothing new on race day. No new shoes, food, or gear. Only things tested in training. Test the gel in a brick workout first.
