Segment Strategy & Gym Simulation
SegmentEffortRace + Simulation
0→200m Tempo
Controlled start. Settle into your stroke within 100m. Sight the first buoy early. Don't sprint.
Pool: 4×50m at 80% effort, 15 sec rest. Practice bilateral breathing and buoy sighting every 6–8 strokes.
Phases 1–4: Build swim base. Phases 5+: Add race-pace 200m sets.
200→550m Steady
Settle and draft. Aerobic effort. Efficient stroke. If you can draft, tuck behind someone's hip.
Pool: 6×100m at steady effort, 20 sec rest. Focus on stroke count per length — lower is more efficient.
550→750m Steady
Finish controlled. Do NOT sprint — save legs for bike. Kick slightly harder in last 100m to activate legs for the beach run to T1.
Pool: 2×100m finishing with 25m of increased kick. Then immediately exit pool and walk briskly 1 min (simulates beach run to T1).
Bike — 12.5 mi Out & Back
🚴
Exit T1, turn LEFT (south) onto Knoxville Rd. Outbound 6.2 mi is net uphill (+30m). Return is net downhill. Ride at 80–85% effort. The bike builds time, but NOT at the expense of your run legs. Elevation: 135m–200m. Total gain: 287m. Source: 343 GPS points from Strava GPX.

Run — 3.1 mi Out & Back
🚨
IT Band Race Strategy: Outbound 1.55 mi is almost entirely uphill (+50m) — safer for the IT band. Return 1.55 mi is almost entirely downhill (−50m) — the danger zone for IT band compression. Maintain 170+ spm cadence on descent. Shorten stride. Do not overstride downhill. Exit T2, turn RIGHT (north) onto Knoxville Rd.

Race Day Nutrition Timing
Morning −3 hours
Carb-rich breakfast: white rice + banana, oatmeal, toast with almond butter. 1–2 g/kg carbs. Collagen + vitamin C. 500ml water with electrolytes.
Morning −30 min
Small top-up: half a banana or 2–3 dates. Sip water. No large meals.
Swim → T1
Nothing. Get to bike fast. Quick sip of water if needed.
Bike mile 1–2
Small sips of water or electrolyte drink. Settle in before eating.
Bike mile 4–5 (descent)
Take a gel or chews here. Low effort section — stomach can handle it. 20–30g carbs. Chase with water. Will be absorbed by the run climb.
Bike mile 10–12
Finish water bottle. Nothing solid — won't absorb before the run.
Run turnaround (mi 1.55)
Grab water at the aid station. Drink or pour on head. Do not skip.
Finish line
Rehydrate immediately. Protein shake within 30 min. Full meal within 2 hours.
Race Day Rules
1Cadence 170+ spm on the entire run. Especially miles 1.55–2.5 (descent). Shorter steps = less knee flexion = less IT band compression.
2If lateral knee pain hits 1/10 on the descent: walk. Walk 30 sec, retry with shorter strides. If it returns: walk/jog to finish. A slow finish beats a 6-week setback.
3Spin 100+ rpm for the last mile of the bike. Zero resistance. Flushes lactic acid, pre-activates run muscles. Your legs will feel dramatically better off the bike.
4Seated climbing only on the bike. Standing loads the IT band and fatigues glutes faster.
5Gel at bike mile 4–5. The descent is your fueling window. Carbs will be available by the run climb.
6Morning ritual on race day. Heat, lacrosse ball, stretch circuit. Same as every other day. Arrive early enough.
7Nothing new on race day. No new shoes, food, or gear. Only things tested in training.
